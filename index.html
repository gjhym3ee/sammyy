WHAT IS TAEKWONDO?

TAEKWONDO is a Korean martial art that emphasizes striking techniques such as punches and kicks. It focuses on speed, agility, and strength and is frequently utilized for self-defense and competition. Taekwondo also teaches mental and spiritual discipline, as well as respect and endurance. It has grown in popularity around the world, with millions of participants and tournaments at both the national and international levels.

More Info.

PTA

The Philippine Taekwondo Association (PTA) is the national governing body for Taekwondo in the Philippines. It is responsible for the development, promotion, and regulation of Taekwondo in the country. The PTA sets standards for training, conducts competitions, and represents the Philippines in international Taekwondo events.

TENETS OF TKD

The tenets of Taekwondo are important principles that guide athletes in their training and daily life. It serves as the foundation of Taekwondo philosophy and is essential for personal growth, character development, and the pursuit of excellence in both the physical and mental aspects of training. These tenets include courtesy, integrity, perseverance, self-control, and indomitable spirit.

TAEKWONDO (태권도)

○ Taekwondo is a martial art that originated in Korea.

○ In Korean, tae (태) means "to strike or break with foot"; kwon (권) means "to strike or break with fist"; and do (도) means "way," "method," or "art." Thus, taekwondo can be translated as "the way of the foot and fist" or "the way of kicking and punching."

○ It has been recognized as an Olympic sport since 2000.

○ Taekwodno follows a belt ranking system which is used to signify a student's level of proficiency. In the Philippines, the system typically starts with a white belt for beginners and progresses through various colored belts, such as yellow, blue, red, and brown, before reaching a black belt. Each belt level represents a different set of skills and knowledge that the student has acquired through practice and training.

BENEFITS OF TAEKWONDO

1. Physical Fitness: It improves strength, flexibility, and endurance.

2. Self-Defense: Taekwondo teaches self-defense techniques.

3. Mental Discipline: It enhances focus and self-control.

4. Confidence: Achieving higher belt levels boosts self-confidence.

5. Stress Relief: Practicing helps reduce stress and anxiety.

6. Balance and Coordination: It enhances physical coordination.

7. Goal Setting: Progression through belts sets achievable goals.

8. Respect and Discipline: Taekwondo promotes respect for others and self-discipline.

9. Weight Management: It can aid in weight loss and maintenance.

10. Social Interaction: Classes provide a supportive community.

TAEKWONDO COMPETITIONS

POOMSAE

○ (forms)

○ a series of movements, blocks as well and attacks, representing an imaginary fight against one or more opponents. You can do so as an individual or as a group who do the movements at the same time. There are 8 total poomsae for colored belts, and 9 different poomsae for blackbelts.

KYORUGI

○(sparring)

○ is a form of free combat, regulated by rules. Two opponents (HONG-red, CHONG-blue) engage in a controlled combat, using various techniques such as punches, kicks, and blocks, in order to score points. As hits can be made at full power, competitors must be highly protected with protective gears.

KYUKPA

○(breaking)

○It involves the breaking of various objects like wooden board using striking techniques, such as punches, kicks, and palm strikes.

TENETS OF TAEKWONDO

COURTESY

(Ye Ui): Taekwondo emphasizes the importance of showing respect, humility, and kindness towards others. Practitioners are taught to treat everyone with politeness and consideration, both inside and outside the dojang (gym). This tenet promotes harmonious relationships and fosters a positive environment for learning.

INTEGRITY

(Yom Chi): Integrity is the quality of being honest, truthful, and having strong moral principles. In Taekwondo, athleters are encouraged to uphold high ethical standards and to always do what is right, even when no one is watching. This tenet emphasizes the importance of maintaining one's integrity in all aspects of life.

PERSEVERANCE

(In Nae): Perseverance refers to the ability to persist and overcome challenges and obstacles. In Taekwondo, practitioners learn to develop mental and physical strength through perseverance. This tenet teaches the value of hard work, dedication, and never giving up, even in the face of difficulties.

SELF-CONTROL

(Guk Gi): Self-control is the ability to control one's emotions, impulses, and actions. In Taekwondo, practitioners are taught to have discipline and restraint, both on and off the training. This tenet emphasizes the importance of maintaining control over oneself, especially in high-pressure situations.

INDOMITABLE SPIRIT

(Baekjul Boolgool): Indomitable spirit refers to having an unyielding and unwavering attitude, even in the face of adversity. In Taekwondo, practitioners cultivate a strong spirit that enables them to face challenges with courage and determination. This tenet teaches resilience, mental fortitude, and the refusal to be defeated.

Fighting Fear, Finding strength: A Journey by CHAVEZ

Taekwondo introduced me to a new world of discipline and respect that I had never experienced before. My commitment to Taekwondo grew greater as the weeks went into months and years. I trained diligently, learning various forms, practicing kicks, and developing my techniques. With each belt I earned, I felt a sense of accomplishment that was unparalleled. Taekwondo taught me the value of setting goals and working tirelessly to achieve them. As the years passed, I became a black belt, a symbol of my dedication and hard work. Taekwondo transformed me from a timid child into a confident, disciplined, and respectful young adult. I not only excelled in the sport but also in my academics and other areas of life. Taekwondo became more than just a hobby; it became a way of life. I started teaching younger students, passing on the values and skills I had learned. The gym became a second home, and the people there were a second family. I have competed in numerous tournaments, won several medals, and traveled to different places but the real victory for me has been the personal growth that I have experienced through this sport. Today, Taekwondo remains an integral part of my life story. I have learned to push myself beyond my limits and to never give up even when things get tough. It has shaped me into the person I am today, instilling values of discipline, respect, perseverance, and confidence. My journey with Taekwondo continues, and with each new kick, I am reminded that life is a martial art, and with the right mindset, I can overcome any obstacle that comes my way.
